02 — History

Evolution of
Computing

1945

ENIAC — First Electronic Computer

The Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer weighed 30 tons and filled an entire room. It could perform 5,000 additions per second — a marvel of its era.

1971

The Microprocessor Revolution

Intel's 4004 chip placed an entire CPU on a single chip, shrinking computing power to pocket-sized devices and setting the stage for personal computers.

1984

Apple Macintosh & the GUI Era

The graphical user interface made computers accessible to everyday people. No longer just for engineers — anyone could point, click, and compute.

1991

The World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee's invention transformed computers from standalone machines into portals of interconnected global information — the internet as we know it was born.

2007

Smartphones: Computers in Your Pocket

The iPhone merged communication, computing, and connectivity into one device — putting supercomputer power into billions of hands worldwide.

2023+

Artificial Intelligence & Beyond

Large language models, generative AI, and quantum computing are redefining what computers can do — ushering in an era of machine creativity and superintelligence.

03 — Impact

Why Computers Matter

⚡

Speed & Efficiency

Computers perform billions of calculations per second, automating tasks that would take humans years — freeing up time for creative and strategic thinking.

🎯

Accuracy & Precision

Unlike humans, computers don't fatigue or make arithmetic errors. They deliver consistent, precise output whether it's the first calculation or the billionth.

🌍

Global Connectivity

Computers bridged the world — enabling instant communication, remote collaboration, and access to humanity's collective knowledge from anywhere on earth.

📊

Data & Decision Making

Big data analytics allow businesses and governments to make evidence-based decisions, identify patterns, predict trends, and optimize outcomes at scale.

🏗️

Innovation Engine

Every modern innovation — from vaccines to electric vehicles — was designed, simulated, and refined using computational power. Computers accelerate discovery.

♿

Accessibility & Inclusion

Screen readers, voice recognition, translation tools, and adaptive interfaces make the digital world accessible to people of all abilities and languages.
